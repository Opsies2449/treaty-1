Treaty 1

What is treaty 1?

Treaties are agreements made between the Canadian government and the indigenous people who are already living on the land. The agreements were about sharing their land, and their rights.

Treaty 1 is one of the first treaties that was negotiated and was signed in 1871. The area of treaty 1 covered most of southern Manitoba, including cities like Winnipeg and Brandon.

Where was treaty 1 signed?

The treaty was signed in lower fort Garry, also known as the stone fort. It is located near the town of Selkirk, north-east of Winnipeg. The Canadian government chose lower fort garry because it was a good meeting place.

Who were involved?

The indigenous groups that participated in treaty 1 were the Anishinaabe and the swampy Cree. The chief that represented the groups was chief Peguis, and chief Henry Prince.The people that was apart of the Canadian government was Adams Archibald, as the lieutenant governor, Wemyss Simpson as the commissioner, James McKay as the translator, and on behalf of the Crown was Queen Victoria.

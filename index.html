Treaty 1

What is Treaty 1?

Treaties are agreements made between the Canadian government and the Indigenous people who were already living on the land. These agreements were usually about sharing the land and protecting their rights.

Treaty 1 is one of the first treaties that was negotiated and signed in 1871. The area of Treaty 1 covered most of southern Manitoba, including cities like Winnipeg and Brandon, along with other municipalities.

Where was Treaty 1 signed?

The treaty was signed at Lower Fort Garry, also known as the Stone Fort. It is located near the town of Selkirk, northeast of Winnipeg. The Canadian government chose Lower Fort Garry because it was a convenient and strategic meeting place.

Impact

The impact at first

For the Canadian government:

When the treaty is made, the impact for the Canadian government was that they got a lot of land in southern Manitoba, so that they can build cities, railsways, and farmland for more people.

For the Indigenous people:

The impact for the Indigenous people when the treaty was signed was that they had to be moved out of their homes, which lots of it was taken by the government. And they had to live on reserved land with living benefits like farming or health, which most times, the promises was not met.

The impact today

For the Canadian government:

Now in the present, the Canadian government is still responsible for the making of treaty 1. They are responsible for it because they didn't fully keep the promises they made during the signing, and they also did bad things to the Indigenous people like forcing kids to go to residential schools, which a lot of bad things happened there like child abuse, and labor.

For the Indigenous people:

The impacts that was made on the Indigenous people today have still affected them today. The reason for this is because many of them had faced lots of trauma from residential schools
